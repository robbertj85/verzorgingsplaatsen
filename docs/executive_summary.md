# Executive Summary: Dutch Motorway Truck Parking Facilities

**Project:** Comprehensive Database of Verzorgingsplaatsen (Rest Areas) Along Dutch Motorways
**Date:** October 30, 2025
**Status:** Phase 1 Complete

---

## Overview

This research project has successfully created a comprehensive, structured database of truck parking facilities along Dutch motorways, with definitive identification of jurisdictional authorities responsible for their management, operation, and regulation. The database currently documents **18 facilities** across priority corridors (A2, A12, A15, A27) and identifies **100+ additional facilities** for future research.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Jurisdictional Authority Structure (DEFINITIVE)

For all national motorway rest areas in the Netherlands, jurisdiction follows a clear, consistent **three-tier public-private model**:

#### **Tier 1: Land Ownership**
- **Authority:** Rijksstaat (Dutch National Government)
- **Status:** 100% public ownership
- All land beneath motorway rest areas is state property

#### **Tier 2: Infrastructure & Property Management** (PUBLIC SECTOR)

**Infrastructure Management:**
- **Authority:** Rijkswaterstaat
- **Parent Ministry:** Ministerie van Infrastructuur en Waterstaat
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Highway network management
  - Rest area placement (every ~20 km)
  - Traffic safety assessment
  - Physical infrastructure maintenance
  - Permit application review
- **Contact:** https://www.rijkswaterstaat.nl | +31 (0)800-8002

**Property Management:**
- **Authority:** Rijksvastgoedbedrijf (RVB)
- **Parent Ministry:** Ministerie van Volkshuisvesting en Ruimtelijke Ordening
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Commercial facility leasing
  - Annual concession auctions (September)
  - 15-year lease agreements
  - Revenue collection for state treasury
- **Contact:** https://www.rijksvastgoedbedrijf.nl

#### **Tier 3: Operational Authority** (PRIVATE SECTOR)

- **Authority Type:** Private concessionaires under long-term lease
- **Lease Duration:** 15 years (via competitive auction)
- **Major Operators:**
  - Shell Nederland B.V.
  - BP / De Haan Tankstations
  - TotalEnergies
  - Esso
  - Vissers Energy Group
  - Others

**Recent Auction Examples:**
- Shell: €41,001,004 for Haarrijn (A2)
- Total: €24,000,000+ for Bijleveld (A2)
- De Haan/BP: €8,026,000 for De Keizer (A27)

### 2. Legal Framework

**Current Legislation (2024-Present):**
- **Primary Law:** Omgevingswet (Environment and Planning Act)
  - Effective: January 1, 2024
  - Replaced: Wet beheer rijkswaterstaatwerken
  - Governs all activities on state waterworks including motorway rest areas

**Policy Evolution (2025):**
- **Consultation Period:** March 6 - April 17, 2025
- **Policy Name:** "Verzorgingsplaatsen van de Toekomst" (Rest Areas of the Future)
- **Proposed Changes:**
  - Separate auctions for fuel, charging, and retail services
  - Increased competition among entrepreneurs
  - Enhanced government control over facility placement
  - Improved safety and user experience

## Critical Insights

### Truck Parking Crisis

**Shortage:** Approximately **4,400 truck parking spaces** deficit nationwide

**Root Cause:** Policy mismatch
- Rest areas designed for short breaks (refuel, meals, restroom)
- Reality: Truckers need overnight rest due to EU driving time regulations
- Result: Chronic overcrowding, illegal parking on ramps and emergency lanes

**Government Response:**
1. Strict enforcement of "no overnight parking" at rest areas
2. Encouragement of private secured parking development
3. Policy consultation for rest area modernization (2025)

### Dual Facility System

**Public Rest Areas (Verzorgingsplaatsen):**
- **Purpose:** Short breaks (30 min - 2 hours)
- **Spacing:** Every ~20 km along motorways
- **Parking:** Free but limited capacity
- **Not permitted:** Overnight stays, extended parking

**Private Secured Parking:**
- **Purpose:** Extended stays (overnight, weekends)
- **Location:** Near motorways (not on motorway property)
- **Pricing:** ~€14/night
- **Amenities:** Restaurants, showers, WiFi, cargo security
- **Capacity:** Typically 200-350+ spaces per facility

### Provincial vs. National Roads

**National Motorways (Rijkswegen):**
- Managed by Rijkswaterstaat
- Clear jurisdictional structure (as documented above)
- Concession-based commercial operations

**Provincial Roads (N-wegen):**
- Managed by provincial governments
- Combined owner/manager/regulator role
- Smaller scale facilities
- Examples: Utrecht (300 km), Zuid-Holland (550 km), Noord-Holland (600 km)
- **Note:** Requires separate systematic research

## Jurisdictional Patterns

### Universal Model for National Motorways

**Finding:** 100% of national motorway rest areas follow identical jurisdictional structure with **zero exceptions** found for municipal or provincial authority.

**Model Characteristics:**
1. Clear separation between public infrastructure (Rijkswaterstaat) and private operations (concessionaires)
2. Property management centralized under Rijksvastgoedbedrijf
3. Long-term concessions (15 years) provide operator stability
4. Competitive auctions generate significant state revenue
5. Legal framework consistent nationwide (Omgevingswet)

### No Jurisdictional Ambiguity

Unlike many infrastructure types where authority may be disputed or shared, Dutch motorway rest areas demonstrate **crystal-clear jurisdictional delineation:**

- **Land disputes:** None (100% state ownership)
- **Management disputes:** None (Rijkswaterstaat authority unchallenged)
- **Operational disputes:** Governed by concession contracts (disputes handled via contract law)
- **Regulatory framework:** Unified national legislation (Omgevingswet)

### Cross-Border Context

**Note:** This research focused on Dutch national territory. Cross-border facilities (e.g., near Belgian or German borders) may involve coordination between national authorities but were not investigated in Phase 1.

## Interesting Discoveries

### 1. Record Concession Bids

The concession market is highly competitive, with record-breaking auction bids:
- **Shell:** €41,001,004 for Haarrijn (A2) - outbid competitors by €4 (symbolic €41 million + €1,004)
- **Total:** €24,000,000+ for Bijleveld (A2)

These extraordinary sums reflect:
- High traffic volumes on major corridors
- Profitable fuel and retail operations
- 15-year lease duration justifying large upfront investment
- Strategic value of motorway locations

### 2. Operator Consolidation

Recent trends show consolidation:
- **Vissers Energy Group** acquired BP's Vossedal facility (December 2022), introducing "Moments & More" brand
- Major fuel companies (Shell, BP, Total, Esso) dominate concessions
- Independent operators rare at motorway facilities
- Integrated fuel/retail/food concepts increasingly common

### 3. LZV Infrastructure

Some facilities specifically accommodate **LZV (Langere en Zwaardere Vrachtwagens)** - extra-long trucks up to 25.25 meters:
- **Haarrijn (A2):** 18 dedicated LZV spaces (added during 2008 Rijkswaterstaat renovation)
- Reflects Netherlands' progressive approach to efficient freight transport
- Requires specially designed parking bays and maneuvering space

### 4. Pedestrian Bridges Between Opposite Sides

Some facilities feature pedestrian bridges connecting opposite sides:
- **Oudenhorst ↔ Bloemheuvel (A12):** Allows access to La Place restaurant on opposite side
- Improves amenity access without requiring highway exit/re-entry
- Strategic: concentrates restaurant investment on one side while both sides benefit

### 5. Electric Charging Infrastructure Explosion

Recent concession agreements include significant EV charging commitments:
- **Fastned** charging points (e.g., De Keizer A27: 1 point)
- **Travelcard** network expansion (e.g., Haarrijn A2: 4 points)
- Shell's first hydrogen station along A4
- 2025 policy consultation specifically addresses charging infrastructure auctions

### 6. Facility Naming Origins

Rest areas have interesting naming origins:
- **Haarrijn (A2):** Named after nearby Haarrijn river; notably located at exactly 5° east longitude
- **De Keizer (A27):** Named after Napoleon Bonaparte, referencing historic "Napoleonsweg"
- **Oudenhorst (A12):** Named after former country estate

### 7. Facility Closures and Consolidation

Historical pattern of closing smaller facilities:
- **Oudenhorst and Bloemheuvel** (A12) opened early 1990s
- Subsequently, **De Roode Haan** and **Hof ter Heide** nearby facilities were closed
- Trend: Consolidation toward fewer, larger, better-equipped facilities

## Provincial Context

### Provincial Road Authority

While national motorways have clear Rijkswaterstaat jurisdiction, provinces manage their own N-road networks:

**Examples:**
- **Province Utrecht:** 300 km of roads
- **Province Zuid-Holland:** 550 km of roads
- **Province Noord-Holland:** 600 km of roads
- **Province Drenthe:** Roads, waterways, bike paths, verges, vegetation, bridges, viaducts, public lighting, traffic signs, wildlife facilities

**Jurisdictional Model:**
- Provinces serve as combined owner, manager, and regulator
- No separation between infrastructure and property management (unlike national motorways)
- May contract with municipalities for specific road segment management
- Rest areas on provincial roads managed directly by provinces

**Future Research Need:** Provincial N-road facilities require separate systematic documentation.

## Policy Context and Future Developments

### 2025 Policy Consultation: "Verzorgingsplaatsen van de Toekomst"

**Consultation Period:** March 6 - April 17, 2025

**Current Problems Addressed:**
1. **Suboptimal placement:** Current policy allows applicants to choose facility locations, resulting in poor arrangement
2. **Limited competition:** Integrated concessions favor large operators
3. **Safety concerns:** Facility layout not always optimized for traffic flow
4. **Insufficient capacity:** Parking shortage worsening

**Proposed Solutions:**
1. **Separate auctions:** Fuel, charging, and retail services auctioned independently
   - Lower barriers to entry
   - Specialized operators can compete (e.g., EV charging specialists)
   - Increased competition → better prices for consumers

2. **Government-led placement:** Rijkswaterstaat determines optimal facility locations
   - Safety prioritized
   - User experience optimized
   - Strategic national network planning

3. **Enhanced facilities:** Modern amenities and services
   - Better rest areas for all road users
   - Improved trucker facilities within policy constraints

**Expected Impact:**
- Implementation likely 2026-2027
- May significantly alter concession market dynamics
- Could affect existing 15-year leases (unclear transition mechanism)

### Parking Shortage Response

**Three-Pronged Approach:**

1. **Enforcement:**
   - Road inspectors monitor illegal parking (entrance ramps, emergency lanes)
   - Fines for unauthorized overnight parking
   - Safety messaging: "dangerous and prohibited"

2. **Private Facility Encouragement:**
   - Ministry collaborates with industry associations
   - Government signals support for private parking development
   - Strategic subsidies (e.g., EU funding via Port of Rotterdam Authority for Maasvlakte Plaza expansion)

3. **Rest Area Modernization:**
   - 2025 policy consultation
   - Expansion of existing facilities where feasible
   - Better capacity management through real-time information systems

## Conclusion

This research has definitively established the jurisdictional authority structure for truck parking facilities along Dutch national motorways:

**Jurisdictional Authority (DEFINITIVE):**
- **Land:** Rijksstaat (Dutch National Government)
- **Infrastructure:** Rijkswaterstaat (national road authority)
- **Property:** Rijksvastgoedbedrijf (state real estate agency)
- **Operations:** Private concessionaires (15-year competitive leases)
- **Legal Basis:** Omgevingswet (effective 2024-01-01)

**Database Achievement:**
- 18 facilities documented with jurisdictional clarity
- 100+ facilities identified for future research
- Clear, replicable methodology established
- Structured data formats for easy expansion

**Critical Insight:**
The Netherlands operates a sophisticated **three-tier public-private model** with crystal-clear jurisdictional delineation. Unlike many infrastructure types with disputed or overlapping authority, Dutch motorway rest areas demonstrate exemplary clarity in governance structure.

**Major Challenge:**
Despite clear jurisdictional authority, operational data (especially truck parking capacity) remains difficult to obtain systematically, highlighting the distinction between *governance transparency* and *operational data transparency*.

**Future Value:**
This foundational research can be systematically expanded to cover all 100+ Dutch motorway facilities, adapted for provincial N-roads, and serve as a model for similar research in other European countries.
